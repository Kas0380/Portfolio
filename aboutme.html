Numbers have always been my thing. I even earned a distinction in my Master's degree in Mathematics (with honors!). During my academic journey, I published two research papers in international journals, showcasing my ability to draw meaningful conclusions. As a result, I have over 2 years of experience as a Mathematics Tutor and 3 years of research experience. This journey taught me how to break down complex problems and explain them in a clear, concise way.

But numbers alone weren’t enough - I craved the challenge of using them to understand how the world works. I see patterns everywhere. I love analyzing systems, asking “why” and getting deep into what most people overlook. That passion led me to earn Google’s Professional Certificates in Data Analytics and Business Intelligence, and I’ve been building ever since.

In just over a year of internships, freelancing and working in international teams at Data Bridge (Oman), I’ve worked on high-impact projects across domains from sustainability and budget optimization to AI dashboards and admin portals. I’ve secured projects for company, been named "Best Performer" during my internship at CognoRise InfoTech and handled end-to-end development for tools used in real business environments.

I've often taken on projects where I had zero prior knowledge and still delivered beyond expectations. As a former researcher, uncertainty doesn’t scare me, it excites me. The unknown is where I thrive.

I don’t just use tools - I use my mind, creativity and vision to take every project one step further. Over time, I’ve completed 50+ personal and professional projects using SQL, Python, Power BI, Tableau and Excel. These projects included everything from merging messy data from multiple sources, web scraping, A/B testing and sentiment analysis to building dashboards for datasets with over 10 million rows and optimizing customer engagement strategies.

But data isn’t just about numbers, it’s about understanding people, systems and stories. That’s why clear communication is at the heart of everything I do. During a presentation at KultureHire, I was praised for translating complex data into actionable insights and showcasing strong research skills. I’ve also had the honor of presenting my work at international conferences - one of which led to the publication of a research paper!

And because I believe knowledge grows when it's shared, I started my blog, Tiny Data Universe to make data approachable and fun. On top of that, I began freelancing to learn about business firsthand - from onboarding clients and managing scope to building and delivering full websites and data tools. It's helped me grow not just as an analyst but as a problem-solver and communicator.

So, if you're looking for someone who not only understands data but also knows how to think boldly, learn fast and bring fresh ideas to the table — let’s connect.
